Expression systems, such as bacterial, yeast, insect, and mammalian cells, are used to produce proteins or other products from inserted genes. Cloning vectors, which include plasmids, phages, and artificial chromosomes, facilitate the insertion and propagation of DNA segments within host organisms. Together, these tools enable advancements in biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and research by allowing for the creation of genetically modified organisms and the production of therapeutic proteins.</p></p>
