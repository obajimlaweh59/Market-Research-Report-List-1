<p><p>The rare disease treatment market consists primarily of originator drugs and generic drugs. Originator drugs are innovative therapies developed by pharmaceutical companies, often with patent protection, offering unique mechanisms of action for specific rare conditions. These drugs are typically expensive due to research and development costs. The generic market fosters accessibility and affordability, increasing treatment options for patients with rare diseases.</p></p>
