<p><p>Ball screws are critical components in electric brake systems for cars, facilitating precise motion control. The non-circulating ball screw consists of balls that move between the nut and the screw shaft without returning to a storage area, offering simplicity and compactness. In contrast, the circulating ball screw features a looped path for balls, allowing continuous movement and enhanced durability under heavy loads. Both types contribute to efficient braking performance, but their design influences the overall system's size, weight, and reliability.</p></p>
