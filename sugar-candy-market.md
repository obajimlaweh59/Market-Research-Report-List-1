<p><p>The sugar candy market primarily consists of two main types: monocrystalline rock sugar and natural rock sugar. Monocrystalline rock sugar is characterized by its uniform crystal structure, formed through controlled crystallization processes, often resulting in a clear, highly pure product. In contrast, natural rock sugar is derived from evaporation methods, retaining more minerals and varying in color and size. Both types cater to different consumer preferences, with monocrystalline preferred for its aesthetic and purity, while natural rock sugar is valued for its traditional and rustic appeal.</p></p>
