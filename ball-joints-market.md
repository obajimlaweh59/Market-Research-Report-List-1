<p><p>Ball joints are essential automotive components that connect the suspension system to the wheel, allowing for smooth movement. The load-bearing ball joint is designed to support weight and withstand significant stress from vehicle loads and road impacts, ensuring stability and safety. In contrast, non-load bearing ball joints primarily facilitate rotational movement without bearing significant loads, often used in steering mechanisms. Both types play crucial roles in vehicle performance, contributing to overall handling, ride comfort, and suspension durability.</p></p>
