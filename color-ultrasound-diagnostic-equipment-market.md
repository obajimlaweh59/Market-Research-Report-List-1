<p><p>Color ultrasound diagnostic equipment encompasses various imaging technologies, primarily categorized into 2D, 3D, and 4D ultrasound, as well as Color Doppler ultrasound. </p><p>2D ultrasound produces flat, grayscale images, providing basic anatomical details. 3D ultrasound creates three-dimensional images for enhanced visualization of structures. 4D ultrasound builds on 3D capabilities by adding real-time motion, offering dynamic visualization of organs or fetal movement. Color Doppler ultrasound evaluates blood flow and circulation, using color-coded images to assess vessel health and detect abnormalities.</p></p>
